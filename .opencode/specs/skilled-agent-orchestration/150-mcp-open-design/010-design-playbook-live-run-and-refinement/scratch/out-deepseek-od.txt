**EXPECTED DEFAULT (the cliche):** The luxury system codifies the **high-fashion-editorial dark drama** formula: a near-black surface (`#000000`) with white text (`#FFFFFF`) and a stark near-white accent (`#FAFAFA`), driven by Oswald — a bold, condensed, uppercase-display grotesk. This is the look of every luxury-fashion maison's seasonal collection landing page: black background, massive condensed headlines in white, minimal color, dramatic scale contrast, sparse content, heavy reliance on editorial photography. It projects luxury through *intimidation* — exclusivity-by-severity, the VIP section at night. While legitimate for that lane, it is a recognizable AI default ("near-black + one bright accent," per the skill's calibration list) and congeals into the same template regardless of what is being sold.

**PROPOSED DEVIATION: Material-warmth luxury (crafted, not editorial).**

| Axis | Default cliche | Deviation |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| Surface | `#000000` (black) | `#F5F0EB` (warm stone/cream) |
| Text | `#FFFFFF` (white) | `#2C2416` (deep walnut) |
| Primary accent | `#FAFAFA` (near-white) | `#B8860B` (dark goldenrod — metal/gilding, not screen-bright) |
| Display type | Oswald (bold condensed grotesk) | A high-contrast Didone serif (e.g. Cormorant Garamond) — the face of heritage and craft, not editorial speed |
| Signature | Dramatic scale contrast | **Texture as the signature** — subtle linen-grain backgrounds on cards, debossed-press shadow effects on CTAs, as if the page were letterpress-printed, not rendered on glass. The page feels like a physical atelier, not a nightclub. |

**Justification:** Most luxury clients spend enormous care on their physical environments (boutique interiors, packaging materials, tactile finishes) but ship a dark-theme screen that could be any competitor's. The deviation grounds luxury in *material warmth* rather than editorial drama — the same shift that separates a Tom Ford runway site from a Brunello Cucinelli or Loro Piana digital presence. It still reads as premium (serif heritage, restrained metallic accent, generous whitespace) but deliberately rejects the dark-condensed-big-headline formula. The one aesthetic risk is the warm-cream surface — it could read as "soft" rather than "exclusive," so the typographic precision and metallic accent must hold the line on luxury. On a free axis, the skill prohibits spending freedom on a listed default; the deviation satisfies that constraint.
