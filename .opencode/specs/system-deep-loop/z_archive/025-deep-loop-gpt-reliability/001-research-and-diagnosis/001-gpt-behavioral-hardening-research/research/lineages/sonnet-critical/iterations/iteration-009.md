## Self-check 1 — Finding 2/6 (native/cli-claude-code as a KQ1 baseline surrogate): overstated ease

Iteration 1-2 characterized running a native or `cli-claude-code` Claude smoke as a straightforward, immediately-available substitute for the "missing native/Claude baseline" in the phase-005 trigger. On reflection, this understates a real methodological gap: the decision-record's trigger implicitly assumes the baseline is run through the **same dispatch mechanism** as the GPT leg (`cli-opencode`-driven, command-owned) so the comparison isolates the *model* as the variable, not the *dispatch path*. Switching the Claude leg to `cli-claude-code` or native Task dispatch changes two variables at once (model AND dispatch mechanism), which weakens the isolation the trigger's "while the native/Claude baseline passes" clause is designed to provide. **Revision:** the finding stands as "a *directionally informative*, immediately-runnable partial check," not as "the missing baseline, solved." The formally clean version (same `cli-opencode`-driven command-owned dispatch, Claude as the model, if `cli-opencode` supports a Claude target) would still need the KQ1 external-shell condition or an equivalent non-self-invoking path, unconfirmed either way in the evidence gathered. This is a real weakening of Finding 2/6, applied by this round to itself.

## Self-check 3 — Finding 12-14 (orchestrate-to-@deep NDP violation): a plausible alternative resolution not fully ruled out

Iteration 5 asserts a structural NDP violation in the literal "dispatch @deep and STOP" reading of KQ4. One alternative this round did not fully investigate: `@deep` may be intended as a **session-level handoff** (orchestrate ends its own turn and control passes to a fresh top-level `@deep` invocation, e.g. via a system-level redirect rather than a nested Task-tool call within the same orchestrate turn) rather than a Task-dispatched sub-agent. Supporting this alternative reading: `@deep` is independently listed as a directly-selectable top-level agent (alongside `@orchestrate`, per this session's own available-agent-types listing: "deep: Deep-loop primary router... Tools: Read, Agent") — i.e., the runtime treats `@deep` as reachable at depth 0 in its own right, not only as something dispatched into. If the "orchestrate → @deep" recommendation actually means "orchestrate detects a deep-route request and tells the system/user to re-invoke at top-level as `@deep`" rather than "orchestrate Task-dispatches `@deep` mid-turn," the NDP concern in Finding 13 would not apply, because no depth-1 hop through `@deep` would ever occur — `@deep` would always run at its own depth 0. **This round could not confirm which reading either prior lineage intended** (glm-max's exact words, "dispatch @deep... and STOP," read more naturally as a Task dispatch, but is not unambiguous either). **Revision:** Finding 13's violation claim should be presented in the synthesis as "the Task-dispatch reading of KQ4 is NDP-unsafe; the session-handoff reading may not be — the implementation phase must pick one explicitly and this ambiguity itself is a finding," rather than as a flat, unqualified defect in the prior round's recommendation. Deliverable 3 (iteration 7's corrected routing rule, which dispatches the resolved LEAF directly and treats `deep.md` as reference-only) remains valid and is NDP-safe under either reading, so the concrete fix proposal is unaffected by this softened framing — only the certainty of the violation claim against the prior round's wording is revised down.
