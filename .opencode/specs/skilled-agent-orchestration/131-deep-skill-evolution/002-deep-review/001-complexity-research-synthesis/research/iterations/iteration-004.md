# Iteration 004: Prompt-Pack Candidate-Generation Pressure

## Focus

Analyze the deep-review prompt pack and agent instructions for candidate-generation pressure. The question was whether the current review surface forces bug-class hypotheses, invariant sweeps, producer/consumer tracing, negative-test searches, and adversarial matrix construction before findings are validated, and how that compares to the deep-research prompt/agent surface.

## Actions Taken

1. Read the current research state, strategy, and iterations 001-003 to preserve the working hypothesis: deep-review validates discovered findings more strongly than it generates candidate findings.
2. Inspected `.opencode/agents/deep-review.md` and `.opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl` with line numbers.
3. Inspected shared review doctrine and checklist references under `.opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/`, especially `review_core.md`, `code_quality_checklist.md`, `security_checklist.md`, `test_quality_checklist.md`, and `fix-completeness-checklist.md`.
4. Inspected comparable deep-research surfaces: `.opencode/agents/deep-research.md` and `.opencode/skills/deep-research/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl`.
5. Checked the deep-review contract asset and state/convergence references where they clarify taxonomy, gates, and optional rich fields.

## Findings

1. The deep-review prompt pack does not force candidate generation before severity validation. It names four review dimensions, three traceability protocol groups, and quality gates as evidence/scope/coverage [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:22] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:26] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:31]. Its explicit adversarial requirement starts only after a new P0/P1 exists: claim, evidence refs, counterevidence, alternative explanation, final severity, confidence, and downgrade trigger [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:39]. The output contract requires findings by severity, traceability checks, verdict, and delta records, but the delta example includes finding/classification/traceability/ruled_out rather than hypothesis, invariant, producer, consumer, or negative-test records [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:76] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:86]. So the prompt pressures reporting and adjudication, not systematic candidate creation.

2. The deep-review agent has strong post-discovery controls, but its discovery step is underspecified. Step 4 asks the reviewer to choose a budget profile, perform 3-5 focused actions, review one dimension, count tool calls, and avoid fabricating findings [SOURCE: .opencode/agents/deep-review.md:153]. Step 6 then requires severity definitions, file-line evidence, counterevidence review for P0/P1, typed claim adjudication, and Hunter/Skeptic/Referee for P0 candidates [SOURCE: .opencode/agents/deep-review.md:174]. That sequence means the agent can validate a candidate rigorously once it notices one, but it is not required to first enumerate same-class bug hypotheses, changed invariants, producer/consumer flows, hostile inputs, or absent negative tests.

3. The shared review doctrine contains the vocabulary deep-review needs, but it is attached to findings, not to search coverage. `review_core.md` says each finding should provide `findingClass`, `scopeProof`, and `affectedSurfaceHints`, with classes including `class-of-bug`, `cross-consumer`, `algorithmic`, `matrix/evidence`, and `test-isolation` [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/review_core.md:74]. That is useful metadata after a finding exists. It does not say every review iteration must produce a candidate matrix proving which classes were searched and ruled out.

4. The strongest producer/consumer and adversarial-matrix language lives in fix-completeness, not candidate generation. The fix checklist says it is for "turning review findings into complete fixes" [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/fix-completeness-checklist.md:10]. It then requires same-class producer inventory, consumer inventory, invariant plus adversarial table tests, full matrix rows, and hostile env variants for different finding classes [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/fix-completeness-checklist.md:16]. Its inventory commands are concrete [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/fix-completeness-checklist.md:36]. But because this checklist is fix-side, deep-review does not currently have to run that same discipline before deciding a dimension is clean.

5. The review checklists offer good bug prompts, but the prompt pack does not promote them into mandatory artifacts. Code quality asks about boundary conditions, contract safety, downstream consumers, and data loss [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/code_quality_checklist.md:54] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/code_quality_checklist.md:73]. Security asks what untrusted input reaches a sink and whether validation is output-context aware [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/security_checklist.md:24], and it includes concurrency/race prompts [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/security_checklist.md:82]. Test quality asks whether failure modes and boundaries are tested and whether changed behavior lacks corresponding tests [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-code-review/references/test_quality_checklist.md:24]. These are discovery prompts, but the deep-review per-iteration contract does not require output rows such as "bug hypothesis searched", "negative test checked", or "invariant preserved/violated".

6. The canonical review-mode contract confirms that the current gate model is taxonomy coverage, not bug-class saturation. Its dimensions are correctness, security, traceability, and maintainability, with correctness checking logic, invariants, state transitions, edge cases, and observable intent [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/review_mode_contract.yaml:42]. Its quality gates are evidence, scope, and coverage; the coverage rule is required dimensions plus required traceability protocols [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/review_mode_contract.yaml:97]. Its convergence signal for dimension coverage is weighted at 0.45 and described as required dimension completion plus protocol coverage stability [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-review/assets/review_mode_contract.yaml:120]. None of those gates require a bug-class hypothesis ledger, invariant sweep table, producer/consumer graph, or negative-test search before a dimension counts as covered.

7. Deep-research's comparable surface is more naturally shaped for candidate generation because it carries questions, focus, and structured observation edges. Its prompt injects research topic, focus area, remaining questions, and last-3-iteration summaries directly into each iteration [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-research/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:11]. Its narrative structure is question-oriented rather than severity-oriented [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-research/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:41]. Its delta stream explicitly allows `invariant`, `observation`, `edge`, and `ruled_out` records [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/deep-research/assets/prompt_pack_iteration.md.tmpl:53], and the agent says ambiguous focus should choose the narrower evidence-backed option [SOURCE: .opencode/agents/deep-research.md:122]. That makes a focused bug-finding research pass more likely to externalize a hypothesis graph, while deep-review externalizes a severity report.

8. The direct answer is no: the current deep-review prompt-pack and agent instructions do not force bug-class hypotheses, invariant sweeps, producer/consumer tracing, negative-test searches, or adversarial matrix construction before validating findings. They force scope, evidence, severity, counterevidence for P0/P1, and output consistency. To become less surface-level, deep-review should move the `findingClass` and fix-completeness concepts earlier: each iteration should emit a required candidate-generation matrix before severity validation, with rows for suspected bug class, invariant, producer, consumer, negative/hostile test searched, evidence path, result, and ruled-out reason. P0/P1 adjudication should then validate candidates selected from that matrix, not be the first adversarial step.

## Questions Answered

- Do the deep-review prompt pack and agent force candidate-generation pressure before validation? No. They force disciplined reporting and P0/P1 adjudication after a candidate exists, but they do not require a candidate ledger first.
- Are bug-class hypotheses required? No. `class-of-bug` exists as a finding class in review doctrine, but not as a per-iteration hypothesis-saturation artifact.
- Are invariant sweeps required? No. Correctness mentions invariants, and fix-completeness requires invariant/adversarial tests for algorithmic fixes, but review iterations need not emit an invariant sweep.
- Is producer/consumer tracing required? No. `scopeProof` and `affectedSurfaceHints` can describe producers/consumers for findings, and fix-completeness requires inventories later, but deep-review does not require a producer/consumer matrix to declare a dimension reviewed.
- Are negative-test searches required? No. Test quality prompts ask about failure modes and boundary coverage, but no deep-review state field proves negative tests were searched.
- Is adversarial matrix construction required before findings are validated? No. Hunter/Skeptic/Referee and claim adjudication validate P0/P1 candidates; they do not force candidate construction from a matrix.

## Questions Remaining

- What exact schema should deep-review add: a new `candidateMatrix` field in state JSONL, per-delta `hypothesis`/`invariant`/`producer`/`consumer`/`negative_test` records, or both?
- Should these candidate-generation artifacts be mandatory for all targets, or only for code/spec targets above a complexity or risk threshold?
- Should graph convergence add explicit node kinds for bug class, invariant, producer, consumer, and test, or should post-dispatch validation enforce those fields without graph persistence first?
- How should old packets remain readable while new packets enforce stricter candidate-generation depth?
