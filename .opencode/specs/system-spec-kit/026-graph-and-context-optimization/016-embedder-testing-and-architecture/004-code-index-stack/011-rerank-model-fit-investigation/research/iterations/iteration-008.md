# Iter 8 — Adversarial Synthesis Across Iters 1-7

## TL;DR (2-3 sentences)

The strongest survivor is **path-class boost**, not because it is elegant, but because it is the only candidate whose mechanism directly matches at least two concrete failures (probes 3 and 14). Qwen3 and mxbai remain plausible model-swap candidates, but their evidence is still indirect: benchmark deltas and architecture, not replayed probe outcomes. Hybrid fusion is a cheap experiment with weak causal fit, and prompt injection into bge-reranker-v2-m3 should be treated as ruled out for the current model.

For each candidate, identify the strongest claim against it, contradictions between iters, weak or unsupported evidence, and the adversarial case. End with a ranked-confidence list estimating the probability that each candidate fixes at least two of the four known failure probes (3, 10, 14, 18).

## Findings (numbered, with citations)

### 1. The benchmark target itself is shakier than the packet sometimes admits

The four failures are not equally clean. Probe 3 and probe 14 are clean implementation-vs-test mistakes: a test file beats an implementation target, and the margins are small enough that path-sensitive or stronger semantic scoring could plausibly flip them. Probe 10 is different: iter 2 says the TypeScript source is "semantically more correct" while the fixture expects the compiled `.js` artifact; probe 18 expects an integration test while a reference doc wins, which makes "implementation over docs/tests" an incomplete rule. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-002.md" lines="43-68" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="90-95" />

Adversarial implication: a candidate can "fix" the benchmark by satisfying stale fixture expectations, or improve semantic retrieval while still failing the fixture. Iter 2's broad claim that all four failures are the same lexical-cue-density failure is directionally useful, but it overcompresses two fixture-truth problems into one model-bias story. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-002.md" lines="70-86" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="118-126" />

### 2. mxbai swap: plausible, but the direct evidence is still missing

The strongest claim for mxbai is architectural: it is a Qwen-2.5 causal LM reranker with binary-token scoring and an RL/preference-learning story, and it reports better CoIR code-search performance than bge-reranker-v2-m3. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-003.md" lines="18-18" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-003.md" lines="125-149" />

The strongest claim against it is simpler: no one has run mxbai on probes 3/10/14/18, its specific training data is not documented, and it still scores content without path-class metadata. Iter 3 acknowledges all three points, which downgrades the 70-80% claim from "estimated outcome" to "educated prior." <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-003.md" lines="75-83" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-003.md" lines="144-161" />

Adversarial case: mxbai may improve general code relevance but still rank a lexically dense test/doc above a semantically intended implementation when the candidate content alone carries the decision. It may also "fix" probe 10 in the wrong direction by keeping the semantically better source file above the expected dist artifact. Current probability of fixing at least two of four probes: **0.58**.

### 3. Qwen3 swap: strongest model-swap case, but not clean enough to ship without replay

Qwen3 has the strongest model-swap case because it is explicitly instruction-aware, has a generative reranker scoring design, and reports a large MTEB-Code advantage over bge-reranker-v2-m3. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-004.md" lines="36-46" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-004.md" lines="91-99" />

The best argument against it is integration and evidence quality. Iter 4 says CocoIndex would need chat-template formatting, custom instruction support, and yes/no token logit handling to fully leverage the model; it also admits default prompting can work, but that is weaker than the custom code-search-instruction story. Training-data details, latency, and probe-level validation are all missing. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-004.md" lines="73-81" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-004.md" lines="141-158" />

There is also a cross-iter comparison problem: iter 4 implicitly treats Qwen3's MTEB-Code score as stronger than mxbai's CoIR score, but those are not the same benchmark. That supports "Qwen3 is promising" but not "Qwen3 beats mxbai on this packet's failure distribution." <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-004.md" lines="99-108" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-003.md" lines="108-123" />

Adversarial case: Qwen3 may need the exact prompt integration that has not been implemented; with default web-search instructions, it may improve semantic matching but still miss fixture-specific targets for probes 10 and 18. Current probability of fixing at least two of four probes: **0.66**.

### 4. Path-class boost: best direct fit, but the earlier "all four" framing is too strong

Path-class boost has the best causal alignment with observed failures. The code already has path classification and QueryResult metadata, and the proposed slot is after reranker score prediction but before sorting. Iter 5 also shows probes 3 and 14 should be particularly amenable because tests outrank implementation files by tight margins. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="18-18" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="88-95" />

The strongest claim against it is not implementation feasibility; it is correctness. Iter 5 says "all 4 failure probes have path-class mismatches," but then immediately concedes expected lift is high only for probes 3 and 14 and uncertain for probes 10 and 18. Controls are not profiled, boost factors are heuristic, "reference/" classification is incomplete, and user intent detection is missing. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="95-105" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-005.md" lines="118-126" />

Adversarial case: a static path prior will create false negatives when the user is actually looking for tests, docs, generated artifacts, or reference material. It can also hide fixture problems by bending results toward implementation files. Still, for the narrow target "fix at least two of four failure probes," it has the most direct support. Current probability: **0.72**.

### 5. Hybrid fusion recalibration: cheap, but causally weak

Fusion recalibration is operationally attractive because the weights already exist as environment variables. The adversarial problem is that iter 6 itself argues the failure occurs after fusion: the reranker takes the fused candidate set and then applies content-only cross-encoder scoring. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-006.md" lines="18-18" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-006.md" lines="66-76" />

The unsupported part is channel attribution. There is no dense-vs-BM25 breakdown for the four failures, so dense-heavy and sparse-heavy settings are both speculative. A global weight change also risks degrading unrelated queries, and non-RRF methods are not currently implemented. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-006.md" lines="78-95" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-006.md" lines="132-153" />

Adversarial case: fusion recalibration might remove a distractor from the top-20 window, but if the distractor remains in the window, the same biased reranker can still select it. This is a candidate-set shaping experiment, not a direct fix. Current probability of fixing at least two probes: **0.24**.

### 6. Prompt injection into bge-reranker-v2-m3: effectively ruled out

Prompt injection is the weakest candidate. Iter 7 shows bge-reranker-v2-m3 is a traditional XLM-RoBERTa cross-encoder without instruction prompting, and that the instruction-aware format belongs to other LLM-based rerankers. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-007.md" lines="36-62" />

The contradiction is mostly with the initial hope embedded in the RQ, not with later evidence: the implementation slot is easy, but the model has no documented mechanism to obey the prefix. Iter 7's "no experimental validation needed" is slightly overconfident as a research posture, but not enough to rescue the candidate; at best, a prefix might perturb lexical scoring unpredictably, which is not the same as instruction following. <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-007.md" lines="80-120" /> <ref_file file="/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Code_Environment/Public/.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/016-embedder-testing-and-architecture/004-code-index-stack/011-rerank-model-fit-investigation/research/iterations/iteration-007.md" lines="122-132" />

Adversarial case: the prefix adds noise, biases against legitimate test/doc searches, and still leaves the non-instruction-tuned cross-encoder optimizing lexical pair relevance. Current probability of fixing at least two probes: **0.06**.
