## GAPS

1. **The 008 compiled-contract swap replaces one indirection class with another — the source/compiled drift class — and names no enforcement contract.**
   - Evidence: `035-gpt-reliability-fixes/008-compiled-contract/spec.md:73` REQ-001 names "checksum drift guards" but never specifies the guard's contract (when it fires, hard-fail vs warn vs manual override), the resolve order between maintained layered sources and the compiled artifact (regen from sources, regen from artifact, symlink, copy), or the recovery path. Synthesis §3 sizes it "M-L with drift-guard machinery" (`034/.../research/synthesis.md:34`). Findings-registry F-035 (line 43) lists "checksum headers + CI drift guard" but offers no per-step semantics.
   - Gap: the drift guard IS the next 14-file chain — maintainers must now keep sources AND the compiled artifact AND the drift CI in sync. A guard that's silent until "the compile fails" becomes a build-stage outage; a guard that warns becomes noise; a guard that hard-fails breaks every CLI branch. Whichever is chosen, failure mode is worse than the indirection it replaced because the indirection was at least inert when broken.
   - Severity: major.
   - Amendment: before 008 lands, ship a written guard contract (enforcement locus, fail/warn UX, recovery command), a single canonical build target (`bin/compile-contracts.<ext>`), and a "compile-only" CI stage that runs on PRs without blocking merge until it has green a baseline week. Drop 008 from the dependency chain and gate it on this contract existing.

   - Severity: major.
