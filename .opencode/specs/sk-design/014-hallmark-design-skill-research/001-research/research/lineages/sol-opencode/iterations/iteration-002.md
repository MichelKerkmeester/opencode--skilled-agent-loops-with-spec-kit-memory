## Contract Comparison

### What shipped `design-audit` does better

- **Evidence and severity are materially stronger.** Hallmark maps taste tells directly to `critical / major / minor`; shipped audit requires neutral observation, exact evidence, user impact, P0-P3 severity, evidence labels, fix, and owner. It also prevents a style tell from becoming P0 unless real task failure or severe accessibility/usability harm exists. [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/014-hallmark-design-skill-research/external/hallmark/skills/hallmark/references/verbs/audit.md:3-16] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/audit-contract.md:36-45] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/audit-contract.md:106-129]
- **The shipped score is broader and safer.** Hallmark's six axes are a generative self-critique with an automatic revision threshold; shipped audit scores Accessibility, Performance, Responsive Design, Theming, and Anti-Patterns, and blocks readiness claims when evidence layers are unassessed. The six Hallmark axes should not replace this contract. [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/014-hallmark-design-skill-research/external/hallmark/skills/hallmark/references/slop-test.md:9-24] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/audit-contract.md:47-97]
- **The shipped procedure treats detections as hypotheses.** Hallmark says every gate must answer no, whereas `ai-slop-check.md` explicitly preserves legitimate brand/system choices and requires evidence, impact, severity, owner, and a fix direction. This is the correct posture for a cross-register audit mode. [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/014-hallmark-design-skill-research/external/hallmark/skills/hallmark/references/slop-test.md:1-5] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/procedures/ai-slop-check.md:21-27] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/procedures/ai-slop-check.md:33-39]

- **Structural fingerprints:** Hallmark separately checks generic page rhythm, centered hero shape, nav, footer, fold fit, and purposeless decoration. The shipped registry has only nine tells and none of those five structural page/chrome checks. The reviewed shipped procedure and production reference cover vague copy and generic imagery but do not name either deterministic tell. [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/014-hallmark-design-skill-research/external/hallmark/skills/hallmark/references/slop-test.md:142-158] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/procedures/ai-slop-check.md:21-39] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/anti-patterns-production.md:68-77]

Shipped production hardening broadly covers long text, `min-width: 0`, states, and localization, but lacks these exact probes. Shipped production hardening requires states, names, keyboard behavior, and focus, but not these five geometry-level checks.
