# Minimalist Grounding Cue

An illustrative cue for the realized minimalist look, refined document-style interfaces with editorial restraint, warm monochrome surfaces, and almost invisible interaction polish, used to name the default and deviate from it.

---

## 1. OVERVIEW

### Purpose

Names the realized look for refined document-style interfaces with editorial restraint, warm monochrome surfaces, and almost invisible interaction polish, so a deliberate move off it reads as a choice.

### Usage

Cite this as the realized default to critique against at the critique step, then take the one justified aesthetic risk away from it. It is a constraint to deviate from, never a fixed template, never a preset to drop onto a brief, and never an option surfaced to the user.

---

## 2. PALETTE

- Canvas: white `#ffffff`, warm bone `#f7f6f3`, or near-white `#fbfbfa`.
- Text: charcoal `#111111` or `#2f3437`, secondary gray `#787774`.
- Structure: ultra-light dividers `#eaeaea` or `rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`.
- Accents are scarce, washed-out pastels: pale red `#fdebec`, blue `#e1f3fe`, green `#edf3ec`, yellow `#fbf3db`.

---

## 3. TYPE

- Pair a clean sans such as Geist Sans, SF Pro Display, Switzer, or Helvetica Neue with an editorial serif for hero moments.
- Hero serifs can use Newsreader, Instrument Serif, Lyon Text, or Playfair Display with tight tracking.
- Metadata and keystrokes use Geist Mono, SF Mono, or JetBrains Mono.

---

## 4. SHAPE AND LAYOUT

- Prioritize macro-whitespace, constrained text widths, and asymmetrical bento grids.
- Cards use exactly-light borders, generous padding from `24px` to `40px`, and modest radii from `8px` to `12px`.
- Primary buttons are solid charcoal with small radii, no shadow, and specific copy.

---

## 5. MOTION AND TEXTURE

- Motion should be barely perceptible: fade plus `translateY(12px)` over about `600ms`.
- Use custom easing such as `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)`.
- Add depth with low-opacity warm grain, subtle line patterns, or soft radial light rather than heavy shadows.
