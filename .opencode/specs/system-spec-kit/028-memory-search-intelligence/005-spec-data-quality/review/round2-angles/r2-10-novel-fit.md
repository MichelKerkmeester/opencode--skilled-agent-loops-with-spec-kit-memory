# r2-10 novel-fit (architecture)

**Angle summary:** The "Novel out-of-the-box GO" tier is presented as one peer family over the shared sweep plus engine, but the substrate splits four ways: only 019 and 024 touch the dq engine/registry/sweep at all, the other five ride distinct mcp_server subsystems (vector cache, hybrid-search, causal graph-lifecycle, quality lib), and even the two that mount stretch the engine past its spec'd per-target pure contract, so most of these novels need their own substrate and the program does not say so.

**Grounding note:** The dq engine substrate does not exist on the live tree. `ls scripts/dq scripts/sweep scripts/detectors` all return No such file or directory and `find scripts -name detector-registry*` is empty, so every engine, registry and sweep claim below is a SPEC-PREMISE (research-only), not a contradiction against built code. I verified three seams against live code where a phase makes a concrete tree claim: `registerLlmBackfillFn` is present at `mcp_server/lib/search/graph-lifecycle.ts:635` with callers only in `tests/graph-lifecycle.vitest.ts:730,742` (unwired in bootstrap, confirming 023's claim), and greps for `refresh_queue` and `embedding_context_version` over `mcp_server/` both return empty (confirming 024 and 020 introduce net-new stores).

## FINDINGS

### F1 [P1] Five of the seven novels never touch the shared sweep or engine, and 026 overstates itself as the foundation they all depend on
- **Evidence (SPEC-PREMISE, with LIVE anchors):** Only two novels register a detector and fold into the sweep. 019 modifies `019-novel-contradiction-detection/spec.md:91-93` (`detector-registry.ts` plus `dq-sweep.ts`) and 024 registers at `024-novel-freshness-decay-queue/spec.md:74,91-93,208`. The other five do not. 020 builds its own scripts under `mcp_server/scripts/sweep/` (`020-novel-embedding-drift-monitor/spec.md:96-97`) and joins the embedding drift-guard set rather than the dq registry (`020/spec.md:119` REQ-006). 021 is "not a registered `fixClass`" and declares "None on `026`" (`021-novel-example-test-generation/spec.md:139,94`). 022 lives in the retrieval path `mcp_server/lib/search/hybrid-search.ts` (`022-novel-context-budget-assembler/spec.md:95-96`). 023 lives in `mcp_server/lib/causal/` and wires the shipped graph scheduler (`023-novel-typed-relation-kg/spec.md:93-96`). 025 lives in `mcp_server/lib/quality/` and files into a host queue (`025-novel-per-doc-quality-slas/spec.md:94-96,138`). Against that, `026-shared-safe-fix-engine/spec.md:214` claims "this engine is the foundation everything in the program depends on", while the authoritative build-order at parent `spec.md` ("Build-Order Dependencies") places only A1, B1 and B2 behind the engine.
- **The architecture gap:** the tier name groups seven phases that share almost no substrate. The dq detector substrate hosts 019 and 024, the retrieval/index substrate hosts 020 and 022, the graph/quality substrate hosts 023 and 025, and 021 is a standalone generator. Treating them as one detector family obscures that the 028 governance rollout must sequence each off-engine novel against ITS real substrate, not behind 026. The individual specs are mostly honest about this, but the parent tier framing and the 026 "everything depends on" line are not, so a reader sequencing the program from the parent map gets the wrong dependency picture. Recommend the parent phase-map annotate each novel with its real substrate owner and 026 narrow its claim to "A1, B1, B2 and the B3/C-class detectors".

### F2 [P1] The two novels that do mount break the engine's single-target pure-runner contract, so even they need a substrate extension
- **Evidence (SPEC-PREMISE):** the engine contract is `runDetectors(target, opts)` over a single target (`026/spec.md:76`), "pure and side-effect-free in report mode" (`026/spec.md:155` NFR-P01) that writes nothing in report mode (`026/spec.md:108` REQ-001), and the B1 sweep is a per-file fan-out that "iterates the A1 detector set ... runs each detector in report mode" over the file corpus (`011-b1-scheduled-dq-sweep/spec.md:199`, `:74`, `:114` `--roots`). But 019's detector is inherently cross-document: it pairs docs gated by the `entity_catalog` table and the causal graph then scores each PAIR with an "LLM entailment check", which the spec itself names "the one net-new piece" (`019/spec.md:65,76,201-203`). And 024's detector "iterates the corpus memories" reading per-memory FSRS `retrievability` (`024/spec.md:200-202`), which is a memory-DB-row domain, not the file-target domain the sweep enumerates.
- **The architecture gap:** a pure `detect(target)` over one file cannot express "generate candidate pairs across the corpus then make a network LLM call per pair", and a file-corpus fan-out does not enumerate memory-DB rows. So 019 needs a pairwise candidate-generator seam plus an IO-tolerant detector contract, and 024 needs a DB-row iteration seam, neither of which the engine or sweep specify. The engine's report-mode purity (NFR-P01) is also violated the moment a registered `detect()` performs LLM network IO. Recommend the engine spec either add an explicit non-pure detector class with its own iteration unit (pair, db-row) or move 019 and 024 to a sweep-adjacent runner and stop counting them as plain registry detectors.

### F3 [P2] Detector and registry file locations contradict across 019, 024 and 026, which endangers the SC-001 single-source-of-truth
- **Evidence (SPEC-PREMISE):** the engine fixes the registry home at `scripts/dq/detector-registry.ts` (`026/spec.md:94`, RESOLVED open question `026/spec.md:223` "settled at .../scripts/dq/"), and SC-001 is "A1, B1, and B2 importing the same `detector-registry.ts`" (`026/spec.md:128`). Yet 019 modifies `scripts/sweep/detector-registry.ts` (`019/spec.md:92`) and places its detector at `scripts/sweep/detectors/contradiction.ts` (`019/spec.md:91`), while 024 places its detector at `scripts/detectors/freshness-decay.ts` (`024/spec.md:91`). That is three different directory conventions (`scripts/dq/`, `scripts/sweep/detectors/`, `scripts/detectors/`) for one detector model.
- **The architecture gap:** taken literally, 019's Files-to-Change creates or modifies a registry at a path the engine does not own, which is a second registry and the exact fork SC-001 exists to prevent. Because nothing is built yet this is a pure spec contradiction with a trivial fix, but it is load-bearing for the single-source-of-truth claim and would split the registry if a builder followed each spec verbatim. Recommend pinning all detector entries to the engine's `scripts/dq/detector-registry.ts` and one detectors directory before any novel is planned.

### F4 [P2] The novel tier silently adds new persistent stores and a queue-on-queue chain with no shared owner and an ordering edge the parent build-order omits
- **Evidence (SPEC-PREMISE, with LIVE anchors):** 020 adds an `embedding_context_version` plus model-id plus normalizer-fingerprint column to the vector record at `mcp_server/lib/cache/embedding-cache.ts` and a backfill over the roughly 2022-row corpus (`020/spec.md:94,97,80`), and that column is absent on the live tree (grep `embedding_context_version` over `mcp_server/` empty). 024 creates a net-new `refresh_queue` table at `mcp_server/lib/storage/refresh-queue.ts` (`024/spec.md:92`), absent on the live tree (grep `refresh_queue` empty). 025 then files into "the freshness decay auto-refresh queue or the B3 `refinement_queue`, whichever host queue exists at build time" and names that a "Hard blocker" (`025/spec.md:80,138`). The parent build-order lists only the engine-before-doors and C2-before-retrieval edges (parent `spec.md` "Build-Order Dependencies") and records no novel-to-novel or novel-to-B3 edge.
- **The architecture gap:** three of the seven novels carry their own persistence (a vector-schema migration plus backfill, a queue table, a consumer of that queue) that the shared sweep plus engine does not own or sequence. 025 cannot build until 024 or B3 ships, an inviolable ordering the authoritative graph does not capture, so a sequencer reading the parent map could schedule 025 before its host queue exists. Recommend the 028 rollout add the 025-after-queue edge and the 020 vector-schema-before-backfill edge, and name an owner for the two net-new stores rather than leaving them implicit inside the novel tier.
