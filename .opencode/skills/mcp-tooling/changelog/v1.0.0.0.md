`mcp-tooling` becomes a parent hub: three flat, independently advisor-routable MCP tool-bridge skills fold into one advisor identity with two workflow packets and one figma transport.

> Spec folder: `.opencode/specs/skilled-agent-orchestration/126-mcp-tooling-parent` (8-phase program)

---

## Why

`mcp-chrome-devtools`, `mcp-click-up`, and `mcp-figma` were three flat, independently advisor-routable skills, all `family: mcp`, sharing a router-and-gating pattern and reaching external tools through the shared Code Mode substrate — but with no structural relationship to each other: no shared registry, no single advisor identity, no hub. This repo's parent-hub canon (already applied to `sk-code`, `sk-design`, `system-deep-loop`, `sk-doc`, and `sk-prompt`) exists to fix exactly this shape.

## What Changed

#### Hub scaffold (`mode-registry.json`, `hub-router.json`, `description.json`, `graph-metadata.json`, `SKILL.md`)

- Three `modes[]` entries: two `packetKind: "workflow"` bridges (`mcp-chrome-devtools`, `mcp-click-up`, both `mutatesWorkspace:true`) and one `packetKind: "transport"` bridge (`mcp-figma`, `mutatesWorkspace:false`, declared on a `transport-axis` extension with a mandatory cross-hub judgment pairing to `sk-design`).
- `routerPolicy.defaultMode: "mcp-chrome-devtools"`; base three router outcomes only (`single`, `orderedBundle`, `defer`); tie-break orders the two workflow modes before the transport.
- `graph-metadata.json` is a new hub identity (`skill_id: mcp-tooling`, `family: mcp`) unioning the three former identities' intent signals, trigger phrases, and outward edges (`mcp-figma depends_on sk-design`, the union of `enhances sk-code`), and recording `mcp-code-mode` as an external cross-skill dependency rather than a dissolved intra-hub edge.

#### Content relocation (`git mv`, full history preserved)

- `mcp-chrome-devtools/*` (41 files) → `mcp-tooling/mcp-chrome-devtools/*`.
- `mcp-click-up/*` (155 files) → `mcp-tooling/mcp-click-up/*`.
- `mcp-figma/*` (40 files) → `mcp-tooling/mcp-figma/*`.
- The three packets' own `graph-metadata.json` files deleted — their independent advisor identities dissolve into the hub's single one.
- Each packet's internal self-references (absolute `.opencode/skills/mcp-<x>/...` paths and relative `../` cross-skill links) rewritten for the new nesting depth. figma's cross-references to the co-located `mcp-chrome-devtools` sibling needed no depth change (both moved down together); its references to the untouched `mcp-code-mode` needed one more `../` level.

#### Repointed consumers

- `.opencode/commands/doctor/assets/doctor_mcp_install.yaml` — `skill_dir`/`install_guide` for the three bridges repointed to the nested paths; the pre-existing stale `mcp-open-design` entry (pointing at a deleted flat skill) corrected to `sk-design/design-mcp-open-design`.
- Reverse graph edges: `mcp-code-mode/graph-metadata.json` (`prerequisite_for`, `manual.related_to`) and `sk-design/graph-metadata.json` (`siblings`, `prerequisite_for`, `manual.related_to`) repointed from the three bridge ids to `mcp-tooling`.
- Advisor routing corpus: 3 `labeled-prompts.jsonl` rows retargeted from `mcp-chrome-devtools` to `mcp-tooling`.
- `.opencode/skills/README.md` and root `README.md` catalog rows/prose updated to describe the hub.

## Excluded By Design

`mcp-code-mode` is a fourth `family: mcp` skill but stays flat standalone infrastructure (ADR-005): it is the live `opencode.json` native-MCP server the three bridges (and other skills) reach through the unchanged `code_mode` registration key, serving manuals well beyond this set. It is never a hub member; the only touch to it is a metadata-only reverse-edge repoint in its `graph-metadata.json`.
