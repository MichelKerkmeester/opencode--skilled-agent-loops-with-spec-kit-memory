# cli-devin v1.0.6.1 — bundle-gate clause broadened to cross-model in SKILL.md prose

**Released**: 2026-05-17
**Status**: Cosmetic-only refinement. Two prose edits in `SKILL.md` to acknowledge that the bundle-gate-aversion finding (verbose constraint language pushes models toward defensive output) now has validated cross-model evidence rather than being SWE-1.6-specific.

---

## Summary

v1.0.6.0 shipped the cross-model validation finding upstream via per-skill changelogs (sk-prompt master `cli_prompt_quality_card.md`, plus four sibling cli-* prompt-quality cards). The cli-devin `SKILL.md` itself, however, still carried two SWE-1.6-only phrasings of the bundle-gate clause — in the §3 default-invocation callout and in §4 ALWAYS rule #12. The clause's truth statement is cross-model, but the wording read like a SWE-1.6-only fact.

This release broadens those two phrasings in place. No new rules. No new content blocks. The SWE-1.6 mandatory contract (sk-prompt-required + pre-planning-required + 5-thought-sequential-thinking) stays SWE-1.6-specific via rule #12 — larger models don't need that structure forced on them. Only the bundle-gate clause inside #12 (and its §3 mirror) gets the cross-model truth.

## Why Option 1 (reword in place) over Option 2 (new rule #15) or Option 3 (no edit)

**Option 1 — reword in place** (chosen): the finding's truth-claim belongs exactly where the current narrower SWE-1.6-only claim sits. The current wording is technically narrower than the validated cross-model finding; fixing in place is the minimum-scope correct change.

**Option 2 — new rule #15 ("Cross-Model Composition Guidance")**: rejected. The new fact is a single broadened phrase, not a new rule. Adding a §4 rule duplicates content that already lives in sk-prompt master `cli_prompt_quality_card.md` §3 and the four sibling prompt-quality cards. The project's anti-pattern table flags "DRY this up for 2 instances" / wrong-abstraction — promoting a phrase to a rule for the sake of structural separation is the same shape of misjudgment.

**Option 3 — no edit**: rejected. The §4 #12 contract gates sk-prompt loading on `--model swe-1.6` only. Non-SWE-1.6 cli-devin dispatches (`deepseek-v4` / `kimi-k2.6` / `glm-5.1`) don't pass through #12, so future callers reading just the cli-devin `SKILL.md` would not see the cross-model bundle-gate guidance from the entry-point prose. Leaving the wording narrower than the truth is the wrong default for a skill body that lives downstream of sk-prompt.
