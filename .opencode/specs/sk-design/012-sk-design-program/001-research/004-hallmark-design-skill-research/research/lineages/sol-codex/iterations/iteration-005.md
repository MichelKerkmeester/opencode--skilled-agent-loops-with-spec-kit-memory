# Iteration 5: Foundations, Responsive, Copy, and Authored Themes

## Focus

This iteration deep-mapped Hallmark's `color.md`, `typography.md`, `layout-and-space.md`, `responsive.md`, `copy.md`, and `custom-theme.md` to sk-design's FOUNDATIONS, INTERFACE, AUDIT, shared token vocabulary, and `/interface:foundations` command. The narrow question was whether each asset contributes a concrete role, constraint, heuristic, or authoring step that the shipped hub lacks.

The comparison treats Hallmark's numbers as hypotheses, not universal design laws. sk-design's Brand-versus-Product register, evidence precedence, semantic tokens, deterministic contrast checks, and content-driven adaptation remain authoritative.

## Findings

1. **Hallmark's color model mostly confirms sk-design rather than extending it; its best transferable detail is the distinction between opaque palette roles and alpha modifiers.** Both systems prefer OKLCH, tint neutrals toward project evidence, assign semantic roles before values, rebuild dark surfaces through lightness, desaturate accents in dark mode, and reject color-only meaning. Hallmark additionally states that named palette colors should be opaque and transparency should be a modifier for overlays and shadows. ## Ruled Out

- Importing Hallmark's universal `3-5%` accent cap, pure-extreme bans, gradient bans, or OKLCH-only rule: sk-design's register and source evidence legitimately permit achromatic, drenched, legacy, and source-faithful systems.
- Treating symmetry as an AI defect: low-variance Product and precision surfaces can earn symmetry; the defect is unreasoned default composition.
- Copying Hallmark's exact responsive widths as universal breakpoints: they are useful verification samples, not content-driven breakpoint decisions.
- Copying Hallmark's exact English headline character buckets or loading-second thresholds as laws: localization, font metrics, container width, and measured latency change the correct boundary.
- Copying the attributed voice-sample text from `copy.md`: repository licensing does not prove Hallmark can relicense third-party quotations.
- Adding Hallmark's 20-theme catalog, diversification stamp, or log to sk-design: the extracted styles corpus and evidence ledger already serve broader provenance and retrieval jobs.

## Dead Ends

- A one-to-one Hallmark theme mapping is not useful for Foundations. The transferable artifact is the authoring-route decision and semantic handoff, not Hallmark's named axes.
- Fixed taste percentages do not survive sk-design's Brand-versus-Product register. Role evidence and rendered proof are the durable abstraction.

## Edge Cases

- Ambiguous input: "custom theme" could mean a new permanent style bundle or one-off authored values. This iteration selected one-off authored values because Hallmark explicitly keeps custom themes out of its catalog and sk-design requires measured versus authored provenance.
- Contradictory evidence: Hallmark says OKLCH only but uses a hex accent in its own palette example; bans pure white as a base but permits it in one custom-theme branch; and labels APCA with a WCAG-style ratio. These details are not copied. sk-design's gamut, contrast-calculator, and evidence contracts remain authoritative.
