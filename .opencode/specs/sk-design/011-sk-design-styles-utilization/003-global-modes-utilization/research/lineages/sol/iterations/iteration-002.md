# Iteration 2: Audit Comparison and Open Design Transport Contracts

## Focus

Define complete, mode-safe contracts for (1) corpus-relative drift, similarity, and provenance evidence inside `design-audit` and (2) corpus-grounded live reads and runs through the Open Design transport. The scope is contract validation and ship order, not implementation or live Open Design execution. The corpus remains comparison/reference evidence; target evidence, deterministic accessibility/performance proof, severity, judgment, and remediation ownership remain separate authorities.

## Findings

1. **`AUDIT_CORPUS_COMPARISON v1` should be a separate worksheet/report lane, not another target-evidence type.** Its input is `{target, intendedAnchor?, comparisonPurpose, corpusGeneration, references[]}`. Every reference carries only style id, artifact/content hash, provenance, rights/use label, selected axes, and selection rationale. Each comparison row records `{axis, targetObservation, referenceObservation, relation: aligned|intentional-delta|unexplained-drift|comparison-unavailable, evidenceLabel, limitation}`. An intended anchor may support a drift finding only when the target's owned system or brief names it; otherwise corpus distribution or nearest references are contextual observations, not defects. This preserves the current requirement to resolve one stable target and keep source/render/design-artifact evidence legible while adding the phase-001 intended-anchor-plus-one-contrast comparison cap. [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/evidence_capture.md:40-66] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/assets/audit_evidence_worksheet.md:29-80] [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/011-sk-design-styles-utilization/001-research-utilization/research/lineages/sol/research.md:134-145] [INFERENCE: schema fields operationalize the cited authority and hydration constraints]

2. **Corpus comparison can inform a finding, but it cannot supply severity, accessibility/performance proof, or remediation authority.** Severity continues to derive from target-observed user impact; a corpus comparison may explain systemic token drift, anti-default prevalence, or an unexplained departure from an intended anchor, but similarity alone cannot raise P0-P3 or any `/20` score. Accessibility retains its seven-layer coverage matrix, and performance above 2 still needs a named numeric metric or explicit `not-assessed`; corpus values prove neither. A resulting accepted finding keeps its confirmed/inferred caveat and routes to `interface`, `foundations`, `motion`, and ultimately `sk-code` through the existing backlog card—the audit still never fixes. [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/audit_contract.md:36-97] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/assets/audit_report_template.md:116-146] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/references/anti_patterns_production.md:49-67] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/shared/sk_code_handoff.md:71-99]

3. **`OPEN_DESIGN_GROUNDING_RECEIPT v1` should be the mandatory design-bearing handshake.** Before a guarded read or run, the paired judgment mode emits `{pairedMode, skDesignGateProof, purpose, targetProjectOrSystem, corpusGeneration, selectedReferences[], allowedInfluenceAxes, prohibitedReuse, briefDigest, noCache:true}`. `selectedReferences` contains IDs, hashes, provenance/use labels, and mode-owned rationale—not `DESIGN.md`, token, component, screenshot, or Open Design payload bytes. The transport then (a) confirms the daemon, (b) verifies live `tools/list`, (c) revalidates the corpus generation before any hydration, (d) reads any Open Design system live, and (e) passes only mode-owned, transformed constraints into the brief and discovery answers. A generation mismatch blocks the design-bearing call until the mode re-queries/reselects and supersedes the receipt; it never silently uses stale evidence. [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/SKILL.md:125-154] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/tool_surface.md:60-98] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/tool_surface.md:143-170] [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/011-sk-design-styles-utilization/001-research-utilization/research/lineages/sol/research.md:130-132] [INFERENCE: the receipt binds the existing pairing/no-cache gates to phase-001 generation refusal]

4. **The return path must reconcile transport output back to judgment and proof rather than treating a completed run as acceptance.** The receipt is extended after the multi-turn build with `{projectId, conversationId, runIds, entryFile, previewUrl, returnedArtifactHashes, liveReadAt, toolSurfaceEvidence}`. The paired mode inspects the live preview/artifacts, classifies each allowed influence as `applied|modified-for-target|rejected`, records target evidence and residual gaps, then attaches that reconciliation to `CORPUS_USE_PROOF v1` and the normal mode handoff. `awaiting_input`, zero files, missing preview/artifact proof, a changed corpus generation, or an unreconciled design delta remains incomplete. Open Design owns transport facts only; the paired mode owns parity and acceptance, while audit may later evaluate the built target. [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/design_parity_transport.md:30-45] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/tool_surface.md:154-170] [SOURCE: .opencode/specs/sk-design/011-sk-design-styles-utilization/001-research-utilization/research/lineages/sol/research.md:172-191] [INFERENCE: reconciliation joins transport provenance to the existing application-proof gate without granting transport judgment]

5. **Ship order should validate the audit lane before live transport integration.** Ranked by leverage, cost, and dependency: (1) audit worksheet/report schema plus fixtures for intended-anchor, no-anchor, generation-mismatch, and unavailable-comparison cases—high leverage, small cost (2-4 engineer-days); (2) deterministic per-axis drift fingerprint and provenance validator—high leverage, medium cost (4-7 days), but only after schema fixtures prevent scalar-score authority; (3) Open Design receipt schema plus offline generation/no-cache/pairing validators—high leverage, small-medium cost (3-5 days); (4) live read/run receipt plumbing and return reconciliation—medium-high leverage, medium cost (5-8 days) and last because it depends on the settled receipt and a running external daemon. This refines iteration 1's envelope-first/audit-before-transport sequence with executable contract slices. [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-audit/assets/audit_evidence_worksheet.md:38-80] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/tool_surface.md:143-170] [SOURCE: .opencode/skills/sk-design/design-mcp-open-design/references/design_parity_transport.md:34-45] [INFERENCE: ordering minimizes schema rework and defers the external-daemon dependency]

6. **Unsafe similarity, copying, and transport-authority paths are contract failures, not optional cautions.** Reject a nearest-neighbor or scalar “genericness” score as proof of poor quality or copying; reject exact token/motif transfer based only on proximity or attribution; reject raw corpus/Open Design payload caching; reject stale-generation fallback; reject one-shot `start_run` completion; reject transport-selected style, transport-issued acceptance, and automatic remediation. Similarity may be a reproducible descriptive observation with named axes and limitations, but copying requires independent evidence and quality remains target- and mode-judged. ## Questions Answered

- How should `design-audit` and `design-mcp-open-design` use the corpus for drift, similarity, provenance, comparison, and transport grounding; which cross-mode ideas should ship first at what rough cost? Answer: use `AUDIT_CORPUS_COMPARISON v1` as a non-authoritative comparison lane and `OPEN_DESIGN_GROUNDING_RECEIPT v1` as a generation-bound, no-cache, mandatory-pairing handshake with return reconciliation; ship audit schema/fixtures, then drift validation, then receipt validation, then live transport plumbing.

## Reflection

- What worked and why: separating target, comparison, deterministic proof, and transport evidence before designing schemas prevented corpus similarity from leaking into severity or transport acceptance.
- What did not work and why: a single shared “provenance receipt” was too coarse; audit needs comparison semantics while Open Design needs a temporal pre-call/post-run handshake, so one record would blur authority and lifecycle.
- What I would do differently: validate the two schemas with frozen positive and negative fixtures before connecting either to live tooling; this would make generation mismatch, no-anchor comparison, and incomplete-run failures mechanically reviewable.
