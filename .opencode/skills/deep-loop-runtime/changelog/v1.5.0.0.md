# deep-loop-runtime v1.5.0.0

> The dark-flag graduation arc for finding dedup and the fan-out gauges. The finding dedup is scale-validated and made title-aware, and the lag-ceiling gauge becomes a true stall detector. Both fixes are byte-identical when their existing default-off flags are off. Ships from the 028-memory-search-intelligence graduation packets.

---

## Why This Release Matters

The 028 dark-flag graduation benchmark (phase 008, `deeploop-finding-dedup`) put three default-off fan-out capabilities through a production-path benchmark and graduated all three: the near-duplicate finding collapse `SPECKIT_FANOUT_NEAR_DUP_DEDUP`, the lag-ceiling gauge and the progress-heartbeat gauge. The dedup graduated with precision 1.0 and distinct-recall 1.0 and zero false collapses on the 17-record proof set.

A graduate verdict is not a flip. The follow-up work in the 010 and 011 packets stress-tested each graduated capability past its proof set and found two real defects the original benchmark surface never exercised. The 011 opus deep review read the code rather than running the existing tests, and it quantified a dedup title-only false-collapse rate of 0.50 plus a lag-ceiling that fired on healthy load. v1.5.0.0 closes both. Each fix lands behind its existing default-off flag and is byte-identical when off, so the separate graduation flip stays a clean evidence-gated decision and the committed defaults stay 0.

---

## What Changed

### Finding dedup: graduated, scale-validated, then made title-aware

The dedup collapses repeated finding bodies when different fan-out workers restate the same point under different ids and titles, keeping the strongest severity and the distinct conflicting content.

BEFORE: the dedup was dark, proven only on 17 synthetic records. Its content key (`nearDuplicateContentKey` in `fanout-merge.cjs`) excluded the finding title, so two genuinely-distinct findings that shared a body but differed only in their title collapsed into one record. The 011 deep review measured this title-only false-collapse rate at 0.50, three of six distinct points lost.

AFTER: the dedup is scale-validated on a larger 75-finding free-text set across six workers, spanning identical-body restatements, varied-wording restatements, distinct singletons, body-near-miss pairs and title-distinct pairs the 17-record set never stressed. The content key now applies a Jaccard title-overlap gate (`titleOverlap` with `TITLE_DISTINCT_OVERLAP_THRESHOLD = 0.15`, over stopword-stripped title tokens) so two same-body findings collapse only when their title token sets overlap at or above 0.15. Disjoint titles that name different specific subjects share no content token and stay separate, while a legitimate restatement that paraphrases one point shares its subject noun and still collapses. The bucketer (`getFindingBucket`) was made title-aware to match. The measured title-only false-collapse rate dropped from 0.50 to 0, while identical-duplicate collapse held at 7 of 7 and body-distinguished distinct recall stayed 1.0.

### Progress gauges: graduated, and the lag-ceiling turned into a true stall detector

Benchmark 008 also graduated the lag-ceiling gauge (a one-shot fan-out lag warning) and the progress-heartbeat gauge (per-lineage progress events).

BEFORE: both gauge defaults sat at 0. The lag-ceiling metric measured time-since-pool-start, which is queue backpressure rather than a hang signal, so it false-fired on any healthy fan-out wider than its concurrency. A fully healthy 10-wide pool fired at a 1500ms ceiling even though every slot was making steady progress.

AFTER: the lag metric in `fanout-pool.cjs` is redefined to time-since-last-completion-while-work-is-pending, tracked through `lastProgressAtMs` and reset on every settlement. On a healthy fan-out wider than its concurrency the steady completions keep the clock reset, so the gauge stays silent on backpressure and fires exactly once on a real stall. The same healthy 10-wide pool that false-fired at 1500ms is now silent, and the detector fires once on a genuine 5s stall carrying `metric: 'time_since_last_completion'`. Because the metric is now a true hung-slot signal rather than a backpressure drift, the recommended lag-ceiling default is lowered from 5 minutes to 2 minutes. The recommended heartbeat cadence is 30 seconds, observed at roughly 955 records per hour on a 10-wide run against a 1500-per-hour operator-readable budget. The committed gauge defaults stay 0 because flipping them is the separate graduation step with its own test migration, out of scope for these fixes.

## Files Changed

| File | What changed |
| ---- | ------------ |
| `scripts/fanout-pool.cjs` | Lag metric redefined from time-since-pool-start to time-since-last-completion via `lastProgressAtMs` reset on every settlement, emits `metric: 'time_since_last_completion'`, silent on healthy backpressure, fires once on a real stall, byte-identical when `lagCeilingMs <= 0` |
| `scripts/fanout-merge.cjs` | `nearDuplicateContentKey` collapse now gated by `titleOverlap` Jaccard over stopword-stripped title tokens at `TITLE_DISTINCT_OVERLAP_THRESHOLD = 0.15`, `getFindingBucket` made title-aware, off path byte-identical (never calls the title-aware match or bucketer) |
| `tests/unit/fanout-pool.vitest.ts` | Committed pool test migrated from backpressure firing to stall semantics, plus new silent-on-healthy-backpressure and silent-when-off cases |
| SKILL.md frontmatter | version 1.4.0.0 to 1.5.0.0 |

The committed gauge defaults remain 0 (disabled). The recommended production values (heartbeat 30 seconds, lag-ceiling 120000ms) are documented and proven but not enacted. Flipping the zod defaults is the separate graduation step, which must also migrate the committed silent-when-off suites.
