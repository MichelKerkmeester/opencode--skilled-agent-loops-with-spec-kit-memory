# What Changed in the Deep Loop Runtime: Fanout Determinism, Failure Recovery and Continuity

> The Deep Loop runtime is the fan-out and convergence engine under packet 028's deep-research, deep-review and deep-council workflows. It dispatches concurrent CLI lineages, merges their finding registries, gauges the pool, decides when a loop has converged and threads state from one iteration to the next. Going into 028 it could crash on a fresh run, merge the same lineages into a different order across runs, hide an interrupted pool behind no live gauges, drop a failed lineage with no class and no retry, trust a self-reported novelty number the graph never checked and lose its own open questions between iterations. The subsystem came out of 028 as six scoped phase children under `004-deep-loop`: a reducer anchor fix that stops the crash, a fan-out determinism and observability cluster, a fan-out failure recovery cluster, a stop-input corroboration cluster, a continuity threading cluster and one reliability-weighted convergence cluster held NO-GO as a design of record. Every shipped change is additive or a pure correction, default-safe and reversible, and the one cluster that needed a measured signal it did not have stayed unbuilt on purpose. This holds the same discipline as the rest of 028. No deep-loop candidate carries a measured before-and-after number, every leverage estimate is structural inference, so each change ships for correctness and reversibility rather than a promised delta, the same measurement honesty that elsewhere holds a retrieval gain against the prod truncation floor, the route that never cuts below three results before trimming a longer tail at a confidence cliff under a running token budget.

The candidates came out of the deep-loop external-mining research under `research/`, every seam confirmed against current source before a line was written. The narrative below splits the subsystem into its two natural halves: the fan-out layer that dispatches and merges lineages, and the convergence-and-continuity layer that decides when to stop and what to carry forward.

---

## 1. FANOUT DETERMINISM, OBSERVABILITY AND FAILURE RECOVERY

The fan-out half of the runtime, covering the reducer anchor fix (`001`), the determinism and observability cluster (`002`) and the failure recovery cluster (`003`). These three are the orchestration mechanics: making a fresh run reduce at all, making the merge reproducible and the pool observable, then making a failed lineage classified and recoverable.

**Before**

The fan-out runtime is a fire-and-exit batch orchestrator over three modules. `fanout-merge.cjs` merges the per-lineage finding registries, `fanout-pool.cjs` dispatches the concurrent lineages and `fanout-run.cjs` is the lineage worker. A freshly copied deep-research strategy template carried none of the seven ANCHOR marker pairs the reducer keys on, so `updateStrategyContent` in `reduce-state.cjs` threw `Missing anchor section` on the first reduce after iteration one and the loop could not advance. The merge deduplicated findings first-write-wins on an `id||title` key but had no total order, so the same lineages read in arbitrary OS directory order could merge into a different order across runs. The pool exposed no live lag, pending or failed gauges, those numbers were only recoverable post-run from the orchestration ledger. A SIGINT or SIGTERM on a long run killed children silently with no partial summary, and an empty no-new-findings tick was treated as a failure rather than valid convergence. A failed lineage carried no failure class. The upstream timeout-versus-exit distinction was computed in `fanout-run.cjs` then flattened to a bare message in `settleItem`, and every failed lineage dispatched exactly once with no transient-versus-fatal split and no retry. A resume on missing or corrupt state silently defaulted to a fresh init.

**After**

The reducer anchor fix wrapped the seven reducer-owned headings (key-questions, answered-questions, what-worked, what-failed, exhausted-approaches, ruled-out-directions and next-focus) in their ANCHOR marker pairs in the shipped `deep_research_strategy.md` template, so a fresh copy reduces cleanly without altering the reducer regex (Wave-0, commit `738e118751`). The fan-out determinism and observability cluster shipped a hand-written `compareByContentThenId` content-then-id total comparator layered on top of the `id||title` first-write-wins dedup, read-derived `lag`, `pending` and `failed` pool gauges computed from existing pool counters with no new state and a graceful self-stop that flushes a `stopped`-marked partial summary on SIGINT or SIGTERM and treats an empty tick as valid convergence (Wave-0, commit `46812f12a8`). Its Wave-1 tail then added arrival-order property tests that assert the merged research and review registries are byte-identical across lineage arrival-order permutations, with lineage labels and merged metadata arrays sorted at read time so the whole registry is stable rather than only the findings list, plus a default-off near-duplicate merge dedup behind `SPECKIT_FANOUT_NEAR_DUP_DEDUP` that collapses surface-variant findings by normalized body content while keeping same-id different-content records as separate conflict variants. The failure recovery cluster surfaced a bounded failure class over timeout, exit and salvage-miss as a low-cardinality label in `settleItem` plus a per-class rollup in `buildPoolSummary`, a transient-versus-fatal classifier that defaults an unknown signal to fatal so a misclassification cannot loop, single-lineage re-dispatch of only the failed branch up to a durable `max_retries` of five read from the orchestration-status audit so a crash gets no fresh budget, a resume-time orphan-lineage sweep that detects a started-without-terminal lineage and appends an `orphan_requeued` marker and a recover-vs-fresh gate behind an explicit `--require-existing-state` flag that refuses a missing, empty or corrupt resume state instead of silently fresh-initializing.

**Impact**

A fresh deep-research run no longer crashes the reducer. A fan-out merge produces the same order every time and can collapse restated findings into one when a caller opts in, so the distinct-finding count feeding the convergence diversity signal is not inflated by mere restatement. An operator can watch the pool's lag, pending and failed gauges live, and an interrupted run leaves a `stopped` partial summary rather than dying dark. A failed lineage is classified, a transient one is retried within a durable budget a crash cannot reset, and a retry-success is never miscounted as a failure nor a permanent failure ever masked. A resume on a corrupt state file refuses rather than quietly starting over.

**Why**

The anchor fix, the determinism comparator, the gauges, the graceful self-stop and the whole failure classification and retry path are additive corrections to behavior that was wrong or missing, so they ship always-on and verified, none of them touching a schema or the absent reliability signal. The near-duplicate merge dedup changes merge membership and can move the diversity signal that feeds convergence, so it stays default-off behind its flag and byte-identical until a caller opts in. The orphan-reset auto-redispatch leg stayed lease and heartbeat gated, so the shipped scope is detect and marker only, because re-running a live lineage without a lease could double-dispatch real work. Nothing in the cluster depends on the absent reliability signal. Every input is content text, an exit code or a read-derived counter.

## 2. STOP CORROBORATION, CONTINUITY AND THE RELIABILITY HOLD

The convergence-and-continuity half, covering stop-input corroboration (`005`), continuity threading (`006`) and the reliability-weighted convergence cluster held NO-GO (`004`). These decide whether a loop has genuinely converged and what an iteration hands the next one, plus the one cluster that would bound the STOP decision at its root but cannot be measured yet.

**Before**

The convergence verdict in `convergence.cjs` decides STOP-versus-CONTINUE from a fixed-weight blend of structural graph signals. The self-graded `newInfoRatio` was named in the STOP rationale but never consumed by the structured convergence module. The decision was graph-threshold-only and then annotated as allowed pending a novelty agreement the structured module could not see, so the agreement was model-self-adjudicated, unbounded and unaudited. The read-side pool lag gauge was emitted but never enforced, the council cost guards only returned an advisory tuple and never tripped on an oldest-pending lag. A same-id cross-lineage collision kept the first-seen finding and silently discarded the loser's body. Next Focus was whatever free-text the previous iteration hand-wrote in `resolveNextFocus`, never derived from what that iteration actually found, and an iteration carried only the reducer's machine-owned fold of unresolved strategy questions, not its own self-curated thread of open questions. Underneath all of it the convergence volume terms saturated on raw count with no reference to source quality, so STOP was vote-count-floodable, but the reliability field that would bound it was populated nowhere and every input sat at the prior mean.

**After**

Stop-input corroboration added `computeGraphNoveltyDelta`, an independent graph-novelty signal measured from the FINDING, SOURCE and EVIDENCE nodes the loop already loads, and wired it into the structured STOP as a blocking guard. When the decision would allow STOP and the self-report claims low novelty but the graph delta disagrees, the verdict becomes STOP_BLOCKED with a `novelty_self_report_unverified` blocker, and `effectiveNovelty = max(reportedNovelty, graphNoveltyDelta)` so under-reporting can only raise the bar, never lower it. An absent `--reported-novelty` is a strict no-op, byte-identical to today. The same cluster added a configurable `lag_ceiling` tripwire metered against the already-shipped lag gauge, a keep-both path that retains both sides of a divergent same-id collision in canonical content-derived order with a `_conflicts` marker and a default-off in-lineage progress heartbeat. Continuity threading then computed a self-owned carried-forward open-questions block from existing iteration records with no new model call, distinct from the reducer's machine-owned `openQuestions` fold and de-duplicated against it, and made `resolveNextFocus` derive Next Focus from that carried-forward thread or the latest finding before falling back to the first strategy question and then the terminal sentinel, reusing the already-built convergence stop with the blocked-stop precedence preserved and no new convergence primitive added. The reliability-weighted convergence cluster that would bound the flood at its root, a derived reliability posterior, an f64 Beta primitive beside the live integer scorer, a reliability-weighted cap-and-gate on the STOP conjunction, a trust-keyed contradiction quarantine and the rank field and council seat that consume them, was specified in full and then held NO-GO, built nowhere this cycle.

**Impact**

A STOP can no longer be gamed by a self-report the graph contradicts, and a genuinely low-novelty bookkeeping iteration still stops because the delta is scoped to real finding and evidence nodes. An oldest-pending lag now trips an enforceable ceiling rather than sitting in an advisory tuple. A same-id collision keeps both findings with a conflict marker instead of silently dropping the loser. A run carries its own open questions and its next focus forward across iterations instead of rebuilding cold from a hand-written note. The reliability hold leaves a complete, sequenced design ready to build the moment a per-execution success signal exists, rather than a half-built feature with nothing to weight.

**Why**

The novelty audit and its STOP guard, the lag-ceiling enforcement, the keep-both record and the continuity threading are additive corrections, fail-open or backward-safe on absence, so they ship always-on with an absent input proven byte-identical, none of them depending on the reliability signal. What stayed gated in the stop-corroboration cluster is the calibration, not the mechanism. The live novelty floor, the workflow forwarding of the reported ratio and the heartbeat cadence are runtime-only with calibration pending, because the floor that separates a gaming under-report from a quiet bookkeeping tick has to be measured on real snapshot histories the campaign did not have. The reliability-weighted convergence cluster was recorded NO-GO and held intact as a design of record, because no writer populates `metadata.reliability` today so every input collapses to the prior mean and the weighting layer would have nothing to weight, its threshold recalibration would be meaningless under the all-prior-mean regime and there is no benchmark to prove the signal out-earns the existing confirmation and relevance terms. It waits on the benchmark tier supplying a per-execution success signal and the one benefit micro-benchmark its own plan names as the hard gate, the same discipline that holds every unproven 028 candidate behind the evidence it lacks rather than forcing it through on a structural guess.

## CURRENT STATE

The Deep Loop subsystem shipped its always-on corrections and holds its gated tail, all on the `system-speckit/028-memory-search-intelligence` branch. The reducer anchor fix (`001`) and the determinism and observability trio (`002`) landed in the flat 030 Wave-0 at commits `738e118751` and `46812f12a8`, with the arrival-order property tests and the default-off near-dup dedup added as the local Wave-1 tail. Failure recovery (`003`) and continuity threading (`006`) are implemented. Stop-input corroboration (`005`) is implemented in the runtime with its live threshold calibration, its workflow forwarding of the reported ratio and its heartbeat cadence still gated. Reliability-weighted convergence (`004`) is NO-GO, held at completion zero as the design of record for a later revisit.

The always-on members are pure corrections: the anchor fix, the merge total-order, the pool gauges, the graceful self-stop, the failure class and retry, the orphan detect-and-marker, the recover-vs-fresh gate, the graph-novelty STOP guard, the lag-ceiling tripwire, the keep-both record and the continuity thread. The default-off or gated members are the ranking-or-membership-sensitive seams: the near-duplicate merge dedup, the stop-corroboration live thresholds, the in-lineage heartbeat and the orphan auto-redispatch leg. The whole reliability cluster sits behind its benchmark gate. Nothing here introduced a schema migration or a new dependency on the absent reliability signal, and the parent passes `validate.sh --strict`.
