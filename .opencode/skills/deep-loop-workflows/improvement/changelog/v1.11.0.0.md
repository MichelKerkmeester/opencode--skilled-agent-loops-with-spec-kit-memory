deep-improvement is now a **four-lane** skill: this release adds Lane D (non-dev-ai-system-refine), which benchmarks an AI-system packaging and runs a guarded autonomous refine loop with frozen-surface, different-family-grader, held-out-promotion, and worktree-isolation guardrails — all enforced by a packaging-owned `_loop/loop.py` host that the skill-side adapter calls through a thin env/argv shim.

> Spec parent: `.opencode/specs/skilled-agent-orchestration/123-deep-improvement-packaging-refine` (Lane D program).

## What Changed

### Lane D — non-dev-ai-system-refine mode

- **New loop-host mode.** `loop-host.cjs` gained `non-dev-ai-system-refine` in `VALID_MODES` and `LANE_NON_DEV_AI_SYSTEM`. `planInvocation('non-dev-ai-system-refine', args)` fails closed without `--packaging-root` and returns a single step `{ script: 'run-non-dev-ai-system.cjs', args: [...] }`. The existing `agent-improvement`, `model-benchmark`, and `skill-benchmark` plans remain byte-identical. The loop-host vitest suite stays at 21/21 green.
- **Thin adapter script.** `scripts/non-dev-ai-system/run-non-dev-ai-system.cjs` maps loop-host flags (`--fixtures`, `--variants`, `--held-out`, `--samples`, `--proposer-model`, `--grader-model`) to the `ENV_FORWARD` table, decides `--dry-run` (default, zero dispatches) or `--run` (when `--live` is passed), and spawns `python3 _loop/loop.py`. The adapter owns no loop logic — the loop host lives with the packaging under test.
- **Operator guide and contract conformance checklist.** `references/non_dev_ai_system/operator_guide.md` documents the canonical invocation, the five non-negotiable guardrails (frozen scoring surface, independent different-family grader, held-out promotion gate, N-sample averaging, worktree isolation), the seven kill-switch conditions, the pilot evidence from Barter Copywriter, and a nine-item conformance checklist for new packagings.
- **Feature catalog entry.** `feature_catalog/06--non-dev-ai-system/guarded-refine-loop.md` covers the loop contract, the thin adapter seam, guardrails/kill-switches, and the pilot evidence (self-scores inflated ~+6/25 vs independent graders; promotion-accept proven via a synthetic-deficit run; red-team gauntlet 10/10 dispatch-free).
- **Manual testing playbook entry.** `manual_testing_playbook/11--non-dev-ai-system/synthetic-deficit-and-gauntlet.md` documents the dry-run conformance test, the dispatch-free red-team gauntlet (10 attacks: frozen-surface edit, same-family grader, stale lock, plus seven additional vectors), and the synthetic-deficit live promotion test (inject harmful guidance in an isolated worktree, expect a journaled `promote_accept`).

### Command and agent surfaces

- **`/deep:ai-system-improvement` command.** Added in `.opencode/commands/` and mirrored in `.claude/commands/`; resolves `--mode=non-dev-ai-system-refine` and forwards all Lane-D flags to loop-host.
- **`deep-improvement` agent.** Updated agent definition to include Lane D in its mode notes and dispatch surface.
- **`graph-metadata.json`.** Updated to reflect the four-lane structure with Lane D under the active lane index.

## Provenance

Lane D was designed to close the gap that self-reported quality scores are not a safe optimization target — the pilot on Barter Copywriter confirmed that self-grades were inflated by about +6 of 25 versus independent graders. The guarded-refine design (frozen-surface, different-family-grader, held-out-promotion, worktree-isolation) was derived to make autonomous refinement safe enough for live use, with the loop logic owned by the packaging so it can evolve without touching deep-improvement.

## Upgrade

No migration for existing callers. `--mode` defaults to `agent-improvement` when omitted, and the Lane A–C plans are byte-identical. Packaging teams opt into Lane D by implementing the `_loop/loop.py` contract and pointing `loop-host --mode=non-dev-ai-system-refine --packaging-root <path>` at their packaging root. The `--dry-run` safe default (zero dispatches, zero edits) applies until `--live` is explicitly passed.
