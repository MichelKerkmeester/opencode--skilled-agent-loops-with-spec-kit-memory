# Action Punctuation

Actions appear where the page's argument reaches a decision, not as a repeated visual reflex.

## 1. Regions and composition

Compose an opening that establishes the primary task, supporting regions that answer the objections or prerequisites for that task, and one or two decision regions where action becomes appropriate. Section headings frame questions or commitments the bodies resolve; the page rhythm is defined by the distance between meaningful decisions.

## 2. Remaining rhythm axes

- **Divider language:** let action moments create cadence; use other dividers only for genuine topic changes.
- **Button voice:** distinguish primary commitment, reversible exploration, and contextual utility through wording and placement rather than repetition.
- **Image treatment:** imagery supports confidence before a decision and never competes with the active choice.
- **Reveal pattern:** required information appears before its action; optional detail may remain available without blocking the decision.

## 3. Navigation and footer pairing

Navigation exposes the main task without converting every destination into a competing call. The footer offers recovery and continuation after the final decision region, including lower-priority paths that would distract in the opening frame.

## 4. Applicability guard

**Reach for it when:** the page has one dominant conversion or commitment, supporting content can answer specific decision needs, and action hierarchy must stay unmistakable.

**Avoid when:** the product is primarily exploratory, several tasks have equal priority, or repeated action is required for a collection of independent items.

## 5. Responsive-collapse note

The shared responsive gate must preserve action priority, keep prerequisites before commitment, prevent labels from wrapping into ambiguity, and keep recovery paths reachable. This card does not specify sticky behavior, placement values, or narrow-state rearrangement.

## 6. Failure modes

- The primary action repeats after every body and loses consequence.
- Decorative buttons stand in for navigation or plain informational links.
- A commitment appears before price, risk, requirements, or other necessary context.
- Multiple visually equal actions make the intended decision unknowable.

## 7. Evidence and diversification stamp

Visible proof maps each action to the question or prerequisite resolved immediately before it and demonstrates a single dominant commitment. Record `cardId: "action-punctuation"` in the shared `structuralFingerprintSelections` envelope with button-voice emphasis, decision regions, responsive-gate evidence, and the verified distinction between commitment, exploration, and utility actions.
