## Analysis

**The angle in two halves:**

**1. Feedback auto-promotion — MITIGATED.** `auto-promotion.ts` enforces `source_kind` in three independent places: `checkAutoPromotion` (line 178), `scanForPromotions` (line 354), and — critically — the `executeAutoPromotion` UPDATE carries a concurrent guard in its WHERE clause (`source_kind ... NOT IN ('human','manual')`, lines 277-278) inside a `BEGIN IMMEDIATE` transaction. A manual record cannot be auto-promoted even under a race. The test `source-kind-safety.vitest.ts:75-115` confirms the contract. No gap here.

**2. Same-path reindex-retire — REAL, PARTIALLY MITIGATED.** This is the live defect. The lineage primitive `retirePredecessorForActiveReindex` (`lineage-state.ts:1369`) was deliberately upgraded to **return a `RetiredPredecessorCarry`** = `{importanceTier, sourceKind:'human'}` so callers can re-stamp the successor and keep a human record's overwrite protection across a reindex. The test at `source-kind-safety.vitest.ts:61-62` asserts exactly this contract.

But the carry is consumed by **only 1 of 4 production callers**:

- ✅ `memory-save.ts:2617-2660` — captures `reindexTierCarry`, then `persistSourceKind(database, memoryId, reindexTierCarry.sourceKind)`. Correct.
- ❌ `reconsolidation.ts:332` — calls `retirePredecessorForActiveReindex(db, existingMemory.id)` and **discards the return**, while the merged successor is inserted with a **hardcoded `source_kind: 'system'`** (line 324). Reconsolidation is **default ON** (`SPECKIT_RECONSOLIDATION` default TRUE, search-flags.ts:218-221).
- ❌ `pe-gating.ts:351` (`updateExistingMemory`) — discards the carry, then `applyWriteProvenance` (line 381) re-derives `source_kind` purely from the *new* write's context.
- ❌ `create-record.ts:327` — discards the carry, then `applyWriteProvenance` (line 368) re-derives from the new write's context.

`deriveSourceKindFromContext` (`write-provenance.ts:82-106`) never reads the existing row — it only inspects the incoming write's source/actor/tool/scope. So when an **automated** writer supersedes/merges a **human** same-path record through any of these three paths, `source_kind` flips `human → system`/`agent`. I confirmed there is no higher-level manual-overwrite gate in the save flow (only the carry at memory-save.ts:2655 protects it), and the field-level guard `buildGuardedUpdateParams` (memory-crud-update.ts:100-145) governs *in-place* updates only — the append-version/merge paths bypass it entirely.

**Impact:** the merged/superseded active row keeps the human's `importance_tier` (reconsolidation.ts:297 carries it) but loses `source_kind='human'`. That active row is now auto-promotable and automated-overwriteable when it previously was not — a silent erosion of the documented "automated-writers-never-overwrite-manual" invariant. The human's original row survives deprecated in lineage (no raw data loss), so this is a traceability/correctness defect, not a P0.

Even a human merging an update of *their own* memory yields a `system`-tagged active row — clearly contrary to the carry contract.
