Our process

From green to cup in four honest steps

1

Source

We visit farms and cooperatives directly. No middlemen, no mystery lots. We cup on site before committing to a purchase.

2

Sample roast

Every new lot gets roasted on our sample roaster at least five times before we find the profile that does it justice.

3

Production roast

Batches of 40 pounds or less on our Loring S35. We log every roast and share the curves on the bag label.

4

Rest and ship

We rest every lot 48 hours before packing. Orders ship within one roast day so the coffee arrives at its peak.

How we taste

Every cup has a compass bearing. We label ours clearly.

Floral Fruit Chocolate Spice Origin

We describe coffee the way a sommelier describes wine. No made-up adjectives, no marketing fluff.

Every bag we sell carries a tasting card with the roast date, the roast curve, and three to four flavor notes we actually tasted during cupping. If we write "jasmine" on the bag, it is because three of us independently identified jasmine in a blind cupping.

We publish our cupping scores and invite you to disagree. Coffee is personal, and your palate is the one that matters.
