### TASK ID-001

**Routing:** Design task with free axes — the brief explicitly says "no fixed colors or fonts yet" and "make it feel distinctive." Apply the full two-pass process with the anti-default critique.

**STEP 0 — Ground the subject:** The subject is an artisan letterpress studio — a physical craft medium of ink pressed into paper via metal and wood type. Its audience is people who value tactile craft, archival quality, and the visible evidence of the maker's hand. The page's one job: make the studio's portfolio of printed work feel as irreproducible and material as the prints themselves.

**STEP 1 — Token system:**

- **Color (6 named hex values, drawn from the studio's own materials):**
  - `--printer-black: #1A1A1C` — dense oil-based letterpress ink, not flat #000
  - `--ink-blue: #2B3B4E` — a deep Prussian-inspired ink tone
  - `--type-metal: #7A7D82` — the warm grey of cast lead type
  - `--quoin-copper: #8B6B4E` — oxidized copper from lockup furniture
  - `--rag-paper: #FAF8F4` — unbleached cotton paper, warmer than pure white but not cream-default
  - `--impression-shadow: #E8E4DD` — the faint shadow at the bottom of a deep deboss

- **Type roles:**
  - **Display:** *Franklin Gothic* or a condensed workhorse grotesk with wood-type weight — the face of actual letterpress broadsides. Used at large sizes only, never as running text.
  - **Body:** *Gill Sans* or a humanist sans with letterpress-era provenance — clean but not sterile, carries the studio's factual voice.
  - **Utility:** *Courier Prime* or a slab-serif monospace — for print-run data, edition numbers, and marginalia that echo the language of the shop floor.

- **Layout concept:** *The chase bed.* Content sits in a fixed central column with asymmetric lockup margins (wide left, tight right) that reference the furniture and quoins holding type in a press, not a symmetrical template grid. Sections are separated by horizontal rules that vary in weight — thick for major breaks, hairline for internal divisions — like the leading and furniture in a forme.

- **Signature element:** *The impression hover.* On hover over any portfolio card, a CSS `box-shadow` inset + `transform: translateY(1px)` simulates the paper receiving a deep letterpress deboss — the one thing a letterpress studio can own that no other medium can claim.

**STEP 2 — Anti-default critique:**

| AI-default look | Why I'm moving off it |
|---|---|
| **Cream (#F4F1EA) + serif + terracotta** | This is the *craft default* — the first thing any model reaches for when told "artisan." A letterpress studio's material vocabulary is richer: ink blacks, metal greys, copper patina, and unbleached paper that is cooler and whiter than the warm cream that signals "generic handmade." Using terracotta here would read as a pottery studio, not a print shop. |
| **Near-black + one acid accent** | Reads as tech/startup/DTC. Has zero relationship to ink, paper, or pressure. An acid green next to printer-black would look like a SaaS landing page that accidentally shipped a printing company's logo. |
| **Broadsheet with hairline rules** | The broadsheet layout IS relevant to print (and I keep hairline rules sparingly), but *all* hairlines with zero border-radius and dense newspaper columns reads as a 2018 editorial template, not a working letterpress studio. The chase-bed layout derives from the actual physical structure of a press, which is a deliberate choice vs. the default. |

The quoin-copper accent is the justified risk — a muted oxidized copper, not a warm terracotta. The rag-paper background stays cooler than cream to avoid the craft default.
