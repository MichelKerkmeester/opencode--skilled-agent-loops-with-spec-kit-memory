# Deep Research Strategy: Global Modes Utilization

## 1. OVERVIEW

### Purpose
Determine how the existing 1,290-style library should become useful across the sk-design hub, interface, foundations, motion, audit, and Open Design transport without flattening mode authority or duplicating the phase-001 retrieval architecture.

## 2. TOPIC

How should the 1,290-style design-token library at `.opencode/skills/sk-design/styles/` be utilized globally across the sk-design hub and its non-md-generator modes plus `design-mcp-open-design`, with per-mode integration shapes, out-of-the-box extensions, and rough build costs?

<!-- ANCHOR:key-questions -->
## 3. KEY QUESTIONS (remaining)
- [x] What hub-level contract can expose corpus capabilities, task-derived queries, authority order, and proof requirements while keeping the hub routing-only?
- [x] How should `design-interface` use coherent style exemplars for grounding, anti-default critique, variation diversity, and handoff without becoming a style chooser or copy path?
- [x] How should `design-foundations` turn captured token axes and relationships into safe starting systems, compatibility maps, and implementation-ready evidence without averaging values or claiming accessibility proof?
- [x] How should `design-motion` discover and use sparse temporal evidence, infer no motion from static styles safely, and build motion-specific exemplars or negative baselines under its restraint gate?
- [x] How should `design-audit` and `design-mcp-open-design` use the corpus for drift, similarity, provenance, comparison, and transport grounding; which cross-mode ideas should ship first at what rough cost?

<!-- /ANCHOR:key-questions -->

## 4. NON-GOALS

- Do not design or implement the retrieval engine already settled by phase 001.
- Do not research `design-md-generator`; phase 002 owns that mode.
- Do not modify the corpus, mode contracts, hub, transport, spec packet, databases, or any path outside this detached lineage.
- Do not turn the corpus into a pick-a-vibe menu, preset catalog, or source for unreviewed exact copying.

## 5. STOP CONDITIONS

- Legal convergence requires evidence-backed answers for all five key questions.
- The synthesis must provide a concrete integration shape for the hub, interface, foundations, motion, audit, and Open Design transport.
- Every recommended integration must identify authority boundaries, proof behavior, failure/fallback behavior, dependencies, and a rough build cost.
- At least three smart or out-of-the-box ideas beyond query/cards/hydration must survive contract and risk analysis.
- Stop at 10 iterations even if novelty remains.

<!-- ANCHOR:what-worked -->
## 7. WHAT WORKED
- comparing the phase-001 consumer table directly with each mode's current context, proof, and handoff hooks exposed missing insertion points without reopening retrieval architecture. (iteration 1)
- separating target, comparison, deterministic proof, and transport evidence before designing schemas prevented corpus similarity from leaking into severity or transport acceptance. (iteration 2)
- assigning non-equivalent anchor, contrast, and rejection roles before testing ideas prevented a three-card retrieval result from becoming three selectable presets. (iteration 3)
- comparing the canonical registry text directly with the state/delta value exposed a deterministic identity mismatch, while contract-local negative fixtures tested the answer without reopening live transport or exhausted approaches. (iteration 4)
- comparing the target token starter with both token JSON and rationale made the distinction between structural regularity and semantic compatibility concrete; the typed-edge model emerged from actual cross-axis couplings rather than an abstract synthesis rule. (iteration 5)
- running the restraint gate before defining retrieval separated a correct no-motion outcome from an evidence failure; reading positive and explicitly negative corpus slices then made polarity, purpose, state/scene, and material requirements concrete. (iteration 6)

<!-- /ANCHOR:what-worked -->

<!-- ANCHOR:what-failed -->
## 8. WHAT FAILED
- mode contracts alone cannot validate the proposed schemas against representative corpus bundles; that evidence belongs in focused mode iterations. (iteration 1)
- a single shared “provenance receipt” was too coarse; audit needs comparison semantics while Open Design needs a temporal pre-call/post-run handshake, so one record would blur authority and lifecycle. (iteration 2)
- a scalar “distance budget” could not distinguish intentional target transformation from incoherent drift, so it was replaced by per-axis relational states with reasons. (iteration 3)
- iteration 2's substantive answer used a visually similar but non-canonical question string; exact-string reconciliation made Markdown punctuation load-bearing. (iteration 4)
- treating color, typography, and spacing as independent merely because they are separate top-level JSON keys failed immediately; both references bind those axes through identity, composition, density, and platform assumptions. (iteration 5)
- raw temporal vocabulary and static aesthetic similarity could not distinguish expressive choreography, minimal functional feedback, explicit prohibition, and descriptive use of “transition”; the phase-001 holdout already demonstrates this failure mechanism. (iteration 6)
