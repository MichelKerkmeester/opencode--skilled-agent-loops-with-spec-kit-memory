# Staged Reveal

Complexity appears in an order readers can understand, while core meaning remains available without choreography.

## 1. Regions and composition

Start with the stable frame: task, subject, and expected outcome. Follow with stages that each add one necessary relationship or level of detail, then provide a synthesis region where the full model can be reviewed. Section headings name what becomes knowable at each stage; bodies retain enough context to avoid forcing memory across the sequence.

## 2. Remaining rhythm axes

- **Divider language:** distinguish conceptual stages with quiet transitions; do not make every revealed item a new section.
- **Button voice:** controls say what will be disclosed or changed and preserve a clear return or reset path.
- **Image treatment:** diagrams or media clarify the active stage and retain a static equivalent for essential meaning.
- **Reveal pattern:** sequence follows comprehension or task dependency, supports interruption, and never hides required content behind pointer-only behavior.

## 3. Navigation and footer pairing

Navigation enters the stable frame rather than deep-linking users into unexplained transient states unless those states are durable. The footer appears after synthesis and offers continuation from the complete model, not from whichever stage happened to be active last.

## 4. Applicability guard

**Reach for it when:** the subject has real dependency order, simultaneous detail would overwhelm comprehension, and each stage offers a meaningful checkpoint.

**Avoid when:** content can be scanned more efficiently at once, reveal is decorative, users need comparison across all items, or the sequence conceals costs and constraints.

## 5. Responsive-collapse note

The shared responsive gate must preserve stage order, current-state clarity, keyboard access, static semantic equivalence, and access to synthesis. It owns target-specific transformation and reduced-motion behavior; this card defines no animation or breakpoint recipe.

## 6. Failure modes

- Content is hidden only to manufacture interaction or suspense.
- Readers cannot compare stages or recover earlier context.
- The reveal depends on motion to communicate state or order.
- The page reaches an action before exposing material constraints.
