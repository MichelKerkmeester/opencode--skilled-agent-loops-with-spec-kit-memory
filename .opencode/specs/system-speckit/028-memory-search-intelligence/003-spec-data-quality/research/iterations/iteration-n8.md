# Iteration N8 - Adversarial Prove-First Tiering: MEASURED vs HYPOTHESIS (opus, cross-cutting audit)

## TITLE

Cohort N8 cross-cutting adversarial audit. Model opus. Angle is prove-first. It re-tiers every recommendation surfaced so far on one axis the prior cohorts blurred: not prod-impact-times-feasibility (X1's axis) but **evidence class** — MEASURED-on-this-corpus or externally-validated-and-mechanically-transferable, versus HYPOTHESIS-until-measured-on-this-corpus. The 028 law is invoked as the disqualifier: the brief's external numbers all measure recall@K, which is the exact band the prod 3-result floor truncates away, so an external benchmark cannot promote a retrieval feature here. Every retrieval candidate gets the explicit measurement that would confirm it, and is flagged unproven until that measurement runs in prod-mode.

## FINDINGS

The wave-1 and wave-2 cohorts converged on a real and defensible reordering: adherence/logic/write-time candidates bypass the truncation floor and are the cheap real wins, while retrieval candidates must pay the re-index tax AND prove they reorder the top-3. N8 does not dispute that reordering. N8 attacks the residual optimism that survived it. Three distortions remain.

**Distortion 1: "externally validated" is doing illegitimate work for the retrieval candidates.** The brief (`stage-0-external-findings.md:9-13`) calls prefixing+fusion "externally validated three ways" and quotes hard numbers: Anthropic contextual retrieval cuts top-20 failure 35-49 percent; LLM-metadata-fusion reaches 82.5 percent precision and HitRate@10 0.925; metadata-in-embedding-space lifts Context@5 by 30 points. Every one of these is a recall-or-precision-at-K metric measured at K=5, K=10, K=20. The prod route here truncates to K=3 with a gap-heuristic (`confidence-truncation.ts:35` DEFAULT_MIN_RESULTS=3, applied `:106`). 028 measured the consequence directly: the SAME corpus scored completeRecall 0.212 in eval-mode against 0.036 in prod-mode at K8 — a 5.9x fidelity gap (`028/before-vs-after.md:159`). A +30pp Context@5 win lands almost entirely in the K=4 and K=5 band that prod never returns. So "externally validated" is true and irrelevant in the same breath: the external number is real, measured a metric the prod floor hides, and therefore cannot be cited as evidence the prod top-3 will move. R1 and X1 said this in the RISK section; N8 promotes it from caveat to disqualifier. **No retrieval candidate is MEASURED on this corpus. All are HYPOTHESIS, and the external numbers do not change that tier — they only justify spending the re-index to find out.**

**Distortion 2: the rollup candidate (6/7) is the most over-sold item in the set.** L1, L2, and X2 progressively rehabilitated it from "proven mirage" (X1 ranked it 8th, mirage) to "secondary larger go" (X2). The rehabilitation rests on one true mechanical claim and one untested leap. True: an embedded summary already competes at full cosine with no cap (`memory-summaries.ts` channel), so a first-class embedded rollup is NOT structurally walled by the 0.02 community cap (`memory-search.ts:1286,1290`, confirmed: `communityScoreScale = 0.02`, `calibratedScore = max(0.001, avgMatch * 0.02)`). Untested leap: that a parent-scoped concatenated rollup actually OUT-SCORES the third-best scattered child on a real broad query here. That is pure hypothesis. Worse, the cheap-concat v1 (L2's recommendation) is a keyword-dense template string, and 028 already measured that template-grade community summaries are recall-inert. The mechanism that saves the rollup — first-class embedding at full cosine — is real; the payoff — top-3 displacement — is unmeasured AND carries a live downside the prior cohorts under-weighted: a rollup that wins a broad query by displacing a child ALSO displaces a real specific child on a mis-classified narrow query, and the broad-vs-narrow gate is a heuristic, not a measurement. This is the one candidate where building it risks a net-negative prod outcome, not just a no-op.

**Distortion 3: two candidates are over-engineering against machinery that already exists.** The LLM-as-judge quality score (candidate 5) proposes a per-doc 1-5 Likert score. But `memory_index` already carries a `quality_score REAL DEFAULT 0` column with its own index (`schema-downgrade.ts:80,158,184`; written at `vector-index-mutations.ts:305,356,373`). A second folder-grain score is redundant unless a retrieval consumer reorders the top-3 by it — and none does, so by the truncation law it is a floor-mirage exactly as X1 ranked it (7th, conditional). Adding an LLM call per save to populate a score no ranker reads is the textbook 028 trap plus latency/cost. Candidate 9 (libSQL DiskANN / sqlite-vec RRF) is over-engineering against a shipped system: RRF + sqlite-vec already exist (`shared/algorithms/rrf-fusion.ts`, `sqlite-fts.ts`, `node_modules/sqlite-vec`), so the swap moves nothing measurable and is a pure infra churn risk. Both are NO-GO on over-engineering grounds independent of the floor.

**What survives skeptically.** The schema-validation gate (candidate 4) is the single item that is neither a hypothesis nor over-engineering: the zod schemas already exist (`graph-metadata-schema.ts`, `description-schema.ts`), the wiring gap is real and measured (`validate.sh` checks only `legacy_grandfathered`; R2 corpus-scanned the migration surface: 2022 graph + 2004 description files, 14 graph failures, 0 description failures, 287 docs missing frontmatter, 913 missing the continuity block), and it touches validation not ranking so it has zero prod-retrieval risk. It is MEASURED in the only sense that matters here: its surface and its blast radius are counted, not assumed. EARS+constraints (candidate 3) is a template change that bypasses the floor, but its claimed benefit (reduced ambiguity → better adherence) is NOT measured — the brief itself names the adherence ceiling (`:25`, no format guarantees adherence) and A1/A2 both concede it needs an A/B authoring comparison. So candidate 3 is GO-on-cost (cheap, reversible, floor-bypassing) but its benefit is HYPOTHESIS. That distinction matters: ship it because it is cheap and structurally sound, not because it is proven to work.

## THE UNPROVEN-UNTIL LIST

Each retrieval/benefit claim and the single measurement that converts it from HYPOTHESIS to MEASURED. Until that measurement runs **in prod-mode** (not eval-mode), the claim does not earn a prod keep.

1. **Header-path prefix (1a) improves retrieval** — UNPROVEN until a full re-index runs and eval-v2 dual-mode shows prod-mode completeRecall@3 rises. The brief's 35-49 percent top-20-failure cut is recall@20 and is invisible at K=3.
2. **Metadata fusion (2) improves retrieval** — UNPROVEN until 1a first shows the floor can move AND an alpha sweep is run on this corpus. The 82.5 percent precision and +30pp Context@5 are @K≥5 on non-spec corpora.
3. **Global-ids "carry most signal" here** — UNPROVEN. That is a SEC-10K finding (`:13`); spec packets are not 10-Ks. Must be re-measured if fusion is built.
4. **Rollup node (6) wins broad queries** — UNPROVEN until a real broad query against the live index returns the rollup in the prod top-3 over the scattered children.
5. **Rollup node (6) does NOT harm narrow queries** — UNPROVEN and under-tested by prior cohorts. The narrow-query regression must be measured in the SAME pass, or the rollup ships as a possible net-negative.
6. **EARS/constraints (3) improve agent adherence** — UNPROVEN. Requires an A/B authoring comparison (scope-drift / rework rate, EARS vs prose). The adherence ceiling (`:25`) means structure alone cannot guarantee it.
7. **LLM-judge score (5) improves anything** — UNPROVEN and structurally cannot until a consumer reorders the top-3 by it; redundant with the existing `quality_score` column regardless.
8. **freshness/temporal fields (10) improve retrieval** — UNPROVEN; no scoring path reads them and mtime already computes staleness. Governance value only.
9. **set-merge (7) matters at this scale** — UNPROVEN and premature; full rebuild is cheap (L2) and there is no rollup substrate to keep fresh yet.
10. **DiskANN/RRF swap (9) moves prod ranking** — UNPROVEN and almost certainly null; the substrate is already RRF + sqlite-vec.

The only items NOT on this list are candidate 4 (its surface is counted, its mechanism is validation-not-ranking) and candidate 3's COST (cheap, reversible, floor-bypassing) — though candidate 3's BENEFIT is item 6 above.

## RISK

- **The seductive-external-number trap is the dominant risk to the final synthesis.** The brief's hard percentages read as proof. They are proof of recall@K on other corpora and are mechanically truncated by this system's K=3 floor. If the synthesis cites them as evidence a prod win is likely, it repeats the exact 028 error — promoting on an eval-band metric the prod reader never sees. Guard: any retrieval candidate must carry "prod-mode completeRecall@3 delta, not eval-mode@K" as its explicit unblock condition, and ship default-off behind the re-index gate until that delta is read.
- **The rollup is the one candidate that can go negative, not just inert.** Inert features (5, 9, 7, 10-for-retrieval) waste effort; a mis-gated rollup actively displaces a real specific hit from a narrow query's top-3. Its build is justified ONLY if the narrow-query regression is measured alongside the broad-query win in the same pass. Building it on the broad-query story alone is the riskiest move in the set.
- **Over-engineering risk is concentrated in candidates 5 and 9**, both of which re-implement machinery that already exists (`quality_score` column; RRF+sqlite-vec). Each adds surface and, for 5, per-save LLM latency/cost, for zero measured benefit. These should be cut, not deferred.
- **The schema gate's only real hazard is the frontmatter tier**, not the gate itself: 287 docs missing frontmatter + 913 missing the continuity block means a corpus-wide hard frontmatter gate breaks unrelated packets on day one (R2). Stage frontmatter as warn-first-then-error after a reviewed backfill; the graph+description hard gate is safe after the 14-file sweep.
- **Skeptical bottom line for synthesis:** exactly ONE unconditional GO (candidate 4). ONE GO-on-cost-benefit-unproven (candidate 3). THREE CONDITIONALs that are all HYPOTHESIS-until-prod-measured and must ship default-off behind the re-index + eval-v2 dual-mode gate (1a, 2, 6) plus two governance/enabler conditionals (8, 10). THREE NO-GOs (5 and 9 on over-engineering, 7 on premature/no-substrate). The honest headline: of the ten brief candidates, the data quality program has one proven win, one cheap-and-sound bet, and eight items that are either unmeasured-on-this-corpus or redundant — and no amount of external benchmarking changes that until the prod-mode read runs.
