# Ground the subject

<!-- sk-doc-template: skill_asset_feature_catalog -->

## 1. OVERVIEW

Pins the concrete subject, audience, and the page's single job before any visual choice is made.

This is the first step of the design process. It refuses to choose a palette, typeface, or layout until the work is anchored in a specific subject, so the distinctive choices that follow come from the subject's own world rather than from a generic template. When the brief leaves the subject vague, the skill pins it and states the assumption instead of designing into a void.

## 2. HOW IT WORKS

The step names one concrete subject, its audience, and the single job the page must do, and it states that choice out loud. If the brief already fixes the subject, that framing is used directly. If the brief is thin, the skill pins a reasonable subject itself and surfaces the assumption so the user can correct it, and it escalates when the brief is too thin to ground at all.

Once the subject is named, distinctive direction is drawn from the subject's own materials, instruments, artifacts, and vernacular. Any known context about the user's preferences or prior designs is used as a hint, and the brief's real content and subject matter are carried through the rest of the process rather than replaced with filler.
