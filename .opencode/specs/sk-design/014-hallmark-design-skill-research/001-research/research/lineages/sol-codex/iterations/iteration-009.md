# Iteration 9: Normalized reuse matrix and adoption plan

## Focus

Normalize the prior eight evidence passes into one exhaustive Hallmark-to-sk-design reuse matrix. The selected interpretation of “every asset” is the explicit iteration contract: repository license, skill/verb contract, four verbs, all 24 top-level reference documents, the canonical 58-gate set, pre-emit critique, portable schema, twenty-theme system, nested macrostructure and component catalogs, README, ROADMAP, and every named roadmap proposal. The nested catalogs are covered as two architectural assets rather than 71 near-duplicate pattern rows.

## Findings

1. **Copy-vs-learn verdict: Hallmark is MIT-licensed, but no wholesale copy is warranted.** The license permits use, copying, modification, publication, distribution, sublicensing, and sale, provided the copyright and permission notice accompany copies or substantial portions. Close prose, tables, code, or catalog reuse therefore needs the complete Hallmark notice in a third-party attribution surface; linked imagery, quotations, logos, and other third-party material are not proven redistributable by the repository license. The matrix uses `COPY` only for the legal license lane and otherwise prefers independently expressed `ADAPT`, `LEARN`, or `INSPIRE-NEW`. The surgical route is to strengthen those owners, not mirror Hallmark's public vocabulary.
